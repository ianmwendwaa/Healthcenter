KidneyTreatment

Here at Mwendzi's Healthcare Services, we offer kidney treatment services.
Services we offer paratining your kidney's health:

  1. Blood tests

    2. CKD is a condition where your kidneys can't filter toxins or extra fluid from your blood as well as they should. While the condition can vary in how serious it is, CKD usually gets worse over time. Treatment can slow the progression of the disease. If left untreated, CKD may lead to kidney failure. At this stage, called end-stage renal disease (ESRD), the condition must be treated by dialysis or kidney transplant. Diabetes and high blood pressure are the leading causes of CKD. Analyzing a sample of your urine can reveal abnormalities that point to chronic kidney failure and help identify the cause of chronic kidney disease.

  2. Imaging tests

    3. Glomerulonephritis, for instance, can be resolved by imaging the kidney to determine the level of inflammation.Glomeruli are the thousands of tiny filters that remove waste from your blood in your kidneys. This condition damages them, and your kidneys can't function as well. Inflammation in the glomeruli can happen after a strep infection, as well.

  3. Removing a sample of kidney tissue for testing

    4. Our doctors might recommend a kidney biopsy, which involves removing a sample of kidney tissue. Kidney biopsy is often done with local anesthesia using a long, thin needle that's inserted through your skin and into your kidney. The biopsy sample is sent to a lab for testing to help determine what's causing your kidney problem and make proper prescriptions on the same.

  4. Kidney transplant

    5. When your kidneys fail, treatment is needed to replace the work your own kidneys can no longer do. There are two types of treatment for kidney failure: dialysis or transplant. For many people whose kidneys have failed, a kidney transplant can offer more freedom and a better quality of life than dialysis. To decide about the best treatment for you, you may find it helpful to consult our Nephrological care department. Alternatively, you can choose to talk to people who already have a kidney transplant. Feel free to book an appointment with our specialists.
